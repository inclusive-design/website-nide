CitySpeak- Self Voicing Urban Planning Tool

CitySpeak is a interactive geography learning tool in development at the Adaptive Technology Resource Centre, University of Toronto, that offers students the opportunity to design and construct their own cities.

The current Geography curriculum in Ontario places on emphasis on the practical application of learned skills. In CitySpeak, teachers and students are able to design a starting landscape- complete with a number of different types of terrain and geographical features. This starting landscape can then be transformed into any of a number of different types of cities by placing elements of an urban environment onto the preset map. Students are then required to justify their location and element choices by writing a supporting report for their CitySpeak city. CitySpeak reinforces understanding of elements of geographical features, land-use considerations, urban planning styles and protocols, and community needs and concerns.

Unlike other urban planning tools, CitySpeak is fully accessible interactive learning experience designed to engage all learners regardless of ability.

Accessibility Features of CitySpeak include:

  • ully keyboard accessible
  • Self-voicing
  • High contrast graphics and easy-to-use interface
