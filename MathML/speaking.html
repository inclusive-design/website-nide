MathML Accessibility Project

Speaking Mathematics: Technical Requirements

Coming Soon!
A more explicit paper describing precisely why a MathML reading tool needs to incorporate:
  1. Audio Formatting; to emulate the typical two-dimensional nature of mathematical notation. Audio formatting simplifies the reading process greatly.
  2. User Context Selection: An automated process cannot make assumptions about the meaning of a presentation-based format. A robust reading mechanism must include the potential for a user to define rules for reading shortcuts.
  3. Equation Browsing: Mathematics is too complex to be handled as a passive medium. The user must have a mechanism for breaking up complex expressions, and browsing through them.
The MathML Accessibility Project does not propose to address this third issue at present. The third requirement does not fit into the web-browser/screen-reader paradigm. We note, however, that the first two requirements are requisits for the third, and do not exclude the possibility of expanding to a more active reading paradigm in a future incantation.
